## Q: 
How can different global mythologies be fused dynamically to create modular, evolving narratives that incorporate historical, cultural, and science-fiction elements?

### 1 Core Schema & Structural Layers

| # | Layer | Purpose | Key Objects | Example |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Myth Kernel** | Canonical, cross-cultural “archetype packets.” | _HeroCycle(), CosmicEgg(), TricksterBond()_ | Hindu Prajapati ↔ Greek Phanes ↔ Big-Bang allegory |
| 2 | **Cultural Skin** | Time- and place-specific dressings. | _DynasticPitch(), RitualPalette()_ | Yoruba Ifá skin on CosmicEgg |
| 3 | **Temporal Port** | Historic epoch hooks (ancient ↔ speculative). | _SilkRoadNode(), MarsColonyHub()_ | Trojan War ↔ Interstellar Refugee Crisis 2471 |
| 4 | **Science-Fiction Overlay** | Technology or futurism lens. | _QuantumAncestry(), AI-Pantheon()_ | Norse Ragnarok with self-aware Dyson Sphere |
| 5 | **Flux Engine** | Rule-set for live recombination. | _SyzygyRule(), EntropyBias()_ | “If two kernels contain sibling archetypes, auto-weave.” |

* * *

### 2 Dynamic Fusion Workflow

1.  **Seed**: Select Myth Kernels (JSON/YAML).
    
2.  **Dress**: Apply Cultural Skins (choose epochs & aesthetics).
    
3.  **Bridge**: Insert Temporal Ports to anchor continuity jumps.
    
4.  **Augment**: Layer Sci-Fi Overlays—use hard-science sliders (0–1).
    
5.  **Run Flux Engine**: Procedural rules remix scenes/rituals.
    
6.  **Emit Modules**: Output as scripts, game quests, VR scenes, or transmedia story shards.
